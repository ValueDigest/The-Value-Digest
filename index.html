Our investment criteria

Every stock pitched on The Value Digest must pass each of these fundamental filters. We are looking for businesses trading well below intrinsic value, with strong cash generation, conservative balance sheets, and a clear path to re-rating.

01
Low Price-to-Earnings
We look for stocks trading at a meaningful discount to the broader market and to historical averages. A low P/E is a starting point, not a conclusion — we verify that earnings are real and sustainable.
P/E below 12x, ideally below 8x
02
Price-to-Book below 1x
Buying assets at a discount to their book value provides a fundamental margin of safety. We favour companies with tangible, realisable assets — not goodwill-heavy balance sheets.
P/B below 1.0x preferred
03
Strong free cash flow
Earnings can be manipulated — cash cannot. We prioritise companies generating substantial free cash flow relative to their enterprise value, which funds dividends, buybacks or debt reduction.
FCF yield above 8%
04
Manageable debt levels
Leverage amplifies both gains and losses. We avoid highly geared companies unless cash flows are extremely predictable and stable, and always stress-test the balance sheet.
Net Debt / EBITDA below 2.5x
05
Catalyst for re-rating
A cheap stock without a catalyst can stay cheap for years. We look for identifiable events — management change, asset sale, earnings recovery, sector re-rating — that could unlock value within our investment horizon.
Identified catalyst required
06
Aligned management
Management with meaningful skin in the game make better long-term capital allocation decisions. We favour founder-led or heavily insider-owned businesses where incentives are aligned with shareholders.
Insiders own 5%+ of shares
Our process in practice: A stock must satisfy at least four of these six criteria before we will pitch it. We weight free cash flow and the presence of a catalyst most heavily — a business that is cheap but deteriorating is a value trap, not a value opportunity.
